**Fig. 1.** Optical micrographs of polished wire cross-sections under four
representative heat-treatment conditions. (a) AI alloy after the standard
1200 °C / 1 min anneal: highly refined dual-phase morphology with irregular
grain boundaries (scale bar 0.004 in.). (b) AI alloy after 3-cycle AGG: grain
growth in one phase only; the dual-phase structure persists (scale bar
400 µm). (c) Omori benchmark after 3-cycle AGG: bamboo-like grain morphology
(scale bar
200 µm). (d) Omori benchmark after 1200 °C / 1 min strand anneal: acicular
ferrite microstructure (scale bar 200 µm). All images acquired on a Clemex
Vision PE optical microscope under bright-field illumination.

- Source panels: `Fig2a_AI_1200C_1min_microstructure.jpg`, `Fig2b_AI_3cycle_AGG_microstructure.jpg`, `Fig2c_Omori_3cycle_AGG_bamboo.jpg`, `Fig2d_Omori_1200C_1min_acicular.jpg`

**Fig. 2.** Cyclic tensile stress–strain response. (a) AI alloy after the
standard 1200 °C / 1 min anneal: monotonic loading with no transformation
plateau; essentially zero recoverable strain on unloading; behavior
consistent with dislocation slip. (b) AI alloy after 3-cycle AGG: response
qualitatively identical to (a), indicating that AGG processing did not
restore pseudoelasticity in the AI composition. (c) Omori benchmark after
1200 °C / 5 min strand anneal (larger grain growth): partial pseudoelastic
recovery with developing hysteresis. (d) Omori benchmark after 1200 °C / 12 s
strand anneal (refined small-grain microstructure): pronounced
pseudoelastic hysteresis and a distinct stress plateau. Red arrows in (c, d)
mark the residual unrecovered strain after unloading.

- Source panels: `Fig3a_AI_standard_1200C_cyclic.png`, `Fig3b_AI_3cycle_AGG_cyclic.png`, `Fig3c_Omori_1200C_5min_cyclic.png`, `Fig3d_Omori_1200C_12sec_cyclic.png`
- Assembly note: 2×2 composite, harmonize axis labels and y-axis ranges where possible (panels c–d use 0–8% strain, 0–1500 MPa; panels a–b use 0–10% strain, 0–1500 MPa). Panel labels (a)–(d) in the top-left corner.

**Fig. 3.** Azimuthally integrated synchrotron XRD patterns comparing the
undeformed (0% strain, orange) and deformed (post-tensile-test, blue) states.
(a) AI alloy: pattern indexable to coexisting FCC γ-austenite ({111}γ,
{200}γ, {220}γ) and ordered B2/DO₃ Fe₃Al phase ({220}Fe₃Al, {400}Fe₃Al). The
0% and 10% strain patterns are essentially superimposed: no new reflections,
no extinction of existing reflections; deformation is accommodated by
dislocation slip rather than phase transformation. (b) Omori benchmark: BCC
α-dominated pattern with ordered β-NiAl (B2) precipitate reflections in the
undeformed state. After 8% strain the {111}γ reflection intensifies and the
overlapping {200}α / {200}B2 cluster redistributes in intensity, consistent
with stress-induced γ formation and partial variant activation. Data
collected at SSRF beamline BL12SW with λ = 0.125870 Å.

- Source: `Fig4_XRD_1D_AI_vs_Omori.jpg` (high-resolution composite already includes a/b panels)

---

**Fig. 4.** Two-dimensional Debye–Scherrer synchrotron diffraction patterns
recorded directly on the SSRF area detector for the same four specimen
states. (a) AI alloy at 0% strain: sharp, discrete diffraction spots
indexed to {111}γ, {200}γ, {220}γ on the γ-FCC subpattern and
{220}Fe₃Al, {400}Fe₃Al on the DO₃ subpattern. (b) AI alloy at 10% strain:
ring positions, widths, and azimuthal intensity distributions are
preserved with no detectable redistribution; consistent with the absence
of phase transformation indicated by Fig. 3a. (c) Omori benchmark at 0%
strain: discrete spots indicating coarse, well-oriented grains. (d) Omori
benchmark at 8% strain: spots transition to broader, partially arced rings,
the signature of variant activation and increased mosaic spread
accompanying partial martensitic transformation.

- Sources: `Fig5_XRD_2D_indexed.png` (high-res, indexed 2-panel a/b for the AI alloy), `Fig5_XRD_2D_4panel.png` (4-panel including Omori c/d). Final figure assembly: panels (a)–(d) in a 2×2 layout using the indexed AI panels (a, b) and the Omori panels (c, d) from `Fig5_XRD_2D_4panel.png`, with hkl labels overlaid as in (a).

# Figure resolution and submission notes

| Aspect | Recommendation |
|--------|---------------|
| Minimum resolution | 300 dpi for line art / plots; 600 dpi for micrographs |
| Color space | sRGB |
| Format for submission | TIFF (preferred by SMS) or PDF; PNG accepted for review |
| Width | 89 mm (single column) or 183 mm (double column) |
| Embedded fonts | Arial or Helvetica, minimum 8 pt |

Current source files are at native pptx resolution (typically 300+ dpi for embedded photos and >600 dpi for the chart exports). Composite figures should be assembled in PowerPoint, Inkscape, or Adobe Illustrator and exported as TIFF at 300 dpi or higher for final submission.
